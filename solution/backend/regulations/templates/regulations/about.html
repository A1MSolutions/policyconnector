About

Policy Connector organizes together policy materials from U.S. government websites to make it easier to do policy research. You can use it to:

  • Find materials related to a regulation, including statutes, executive orders, memos, and agency handbooks: 5 CFR 351.801.
  • Find materials related to an executive order or memo, such as regulations, agency guidance, and court cases: Return to In-Person Work.
  • Search across all policy materials: probationary period.

This is an independent open source adaptation of an open source codebase that has had many contributors and versions. For example, check out the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives. The code is in the public domain, and our instance is not supported by or affiliated with any government agency.

How this site is updated

Curated links to resources

To incorporate executive orders, memos, guidance, agency publications, and other kinds of resources, we add links to the database by hand and annotate them with related regulations and executive actions. We make an effort to keep this site up to date, but we cannot promise a specific cadence of updates.

Free public government APIs

This tool displays regulation text from eCFR. When new rules become effective, the Office of the Federal Register incorporates them into eCFR within two business days. This tool syncs with eCFR once a day using the eCFR API. So, it can take up to three business days for changes to be incorporated into this tool.

Links to new rules are pulled automatically from the Office of the Federal Register once a day, using the Federal Register API.

This tool uses the GovInfo link API to provide links to past versions of regulations.

Learn more about APIs and bulk data downloads for U.S. government laws and regulations: Legislative Branch Innovation Hub.
